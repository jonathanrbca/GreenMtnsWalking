Green mountains are walking,
   how do you understand this?
                          ——Dōgen

It all began with an impulsive decision. Towards the end of one of the early COVID waves, and after I had sheltered in place for several months, my twenty-year-old-plus Honda Civic wouldn't start. Well, the heck with it! Who needs a car anyway?

When it came time to go back to in-person work, I stubbornly clung to that decision. I sold the Honda to a co-worker, he plunked in a new battery, and off it went, good as ever.

To go carless, in LA County, when you don't have to, is an eccentric life-style choice, to say the least. There's the Metro, there's bumming rides from friends and colleagues, there's Lyft and Uber. There's breaking down and renting a car for a day or two. There are also bicycling, skateboarding, rollerblading -- not that I have tried any of those yet.

And then there's walking. If you can simplify your life enough (if!), much can be said for walking as a mode of transportation. I've stretched it out to one, two, three, four hours. On these walks, the Palos Verdes Penninsula -- according to geologists a baby mountain, still going up -- is often my companion. To my East, to my South, to my West, it walks with me, marking my progress. Green mountains are walking!, says Zen master Dōgen. One way to understand that!
